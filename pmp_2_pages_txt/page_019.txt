78. What type of chart can track the amount of work done compared to the expected work that should be done in
a project?
OA. Burnup chart
B. Burndown chart
OC. Kanban board
OD. Task board
Correct
a) Explanation: A burnup chart is used to track the amount of work done compared to the expected
work that should be done in a project. It provides a visual representation of progress over time,
showing the accumulation of completed work. By comparing the actual progress against the planned
progress, project teams can assess if they are on track to meet their objectives.
79. What is the Hawthorne effect related to measurement?
A. The tendency to focus on less important metrics rather than the most relevant ones.
OB. The idea that one variable causes another due to their correlation.
C. The influence of measurement on behavior, leading to altered results.
OD. The act of measuring output to encourage a large volume of deliverables.
Correct
c) The Hawthorne effect is the phenomenon where the act of measuring something influences
behavior, leading to altered results. When people are aware that they are being measured or
observed, they may change their behavior, consciously or unconsciously, to match the desired
outcomes or expectations.
80. What is the purpose of an exception plan in project management?
A. To measure project team velocity.
OB. To present up-to-date information for the Planning Performance Domain.
O C. To establish metrics and gather data for analysis.
D. To outline agreed-upon actions when a threshold is crossed or forecasted.
Correct
An exception plan is a set of agreed-upon actions to be taken when a threshold is crossed or
forecasted to be breached. It is used to address potential variances from the plan and to ensure that
the project team is proactive in addressing any expected deviations. The plan helps the team respond
promptly and effectively to changes or challenges that may affect project performance.
81. A project manager is facing ambiguity in the project's schedule status due to unclear language in reports.
Which method can help reduce conceptual ambiguity in project communication?
OA. Reframing the project's objectives B) Implementing a fail-safe mechanism
OB. Implementing a fail-safe mechanism.
OC. Using prototypes to clarify requirements
OD. Formally establishing common rules and definitions of terms
Correct
To reduce conceptual ambiguity in project communication, the project manager should formally
establish common rules and definitions of terms used in reports and communication. This ensures
that everyone involved in the project understands the terms and statements consistently, leading to
effective communication and reduced ambiguity.
