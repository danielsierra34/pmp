51. As a project manager, you are dealing with "scope creep" in your project, where additional scope or
requirements are accepted without adjusting the schedule, budget, or resource needs. Which methodology
can be used to find the appropriate balance for investing in quality prevention and appraisal to avoid defects?
OA. Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA)
B. Earned Value Management (EVM)
OC. Risk Management
D. Cost of Quality (COQ)
Correct
The Cost of Quality (COQ) methodology is used to find the appropriate balance for investing in
quality prevention and appraisal to avoid defects. It identifies four categories of costs associated with
quality: prevention, appraisal, internal failure, and external failure.
52. You are managing a project that requires adherence to certain performance levels in the deliverables. Which
aspect of quality is concerned with the performance levels that must be met in the project deliverables?
A. Prevention costs
OB. Appraisal costs
OC. Internal failure costs
OD. Quality requirements
Correct
Quality requirements are concerned with the performance levels that must be met in the project
deliverables. They are reflected in the completion criteria, definition of done, statement of work, or
requirements documentation.
53. You are a project manager responsible for managing the cost of quality (COQ) in your project. Which category
of COQ includes costs associated with measuring and monitoring activities related to quality to determine the
degree of conformance to quality requirements?
O A. Prevention
B. Appraisal
OC. Internal failure
OD. External Failure
Correct
Appraisal. Appraisal costs are incurred to determine the degree of conformance to quality
requirements. These costs are associated with activities like verification, quality audits, and supplier
rating to measure and monitor quality
54. As a project manager, you want to optimize the delivered value and minimize the costs associated with
quality. What is a good investment to achieve this goal, according to the provided information?
A. Early inspection and review work to find quality issues as soon as possible.
OB. Increasing the budget for external failure costs to handle defects after customer deployment.
OC. Focusing on prevention costs only to avoid quality problems entirely.
OD. Implementing quality controls only during the final stages of the project.
Correct
Early inspection and review work to find quality issues as soon as possible. According to the provided
information, to optimize delivered value and minimize costs associated with quality, early inspection
and review work focused on finding quality issues as soon as possible are good investments. Trying to
"test-quality-in" late in the development life cycle is likely to fail and result in higher costs due to high
rates of scrap, rework, and ripple effects on downstream outputs and stakeholders.
